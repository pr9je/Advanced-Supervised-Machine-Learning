# 1. **Business Understanding**
### Problem Statement
- Scaler capture a sefl-reported snapshot of its learner base: which company they work at, their job position, their current CTC, and the year they started their career. Today this data sits unused as flat rows, there is no systematic way to answer *"what kinds of learners do we have, and what does each group need?"*


### Business Objective
- Segment Scaler's learner base into **data-driven, business-interpretable clusters** so that the course content, career guidance, pricing, and marketing can be **personalized per segemtn** instead of applied uniformly.

### Business Problems We're Solving
  1. **One-Size-fits-all curriculum risk** — a fresher and a 12 year veteran engineer have very different learning needs; a singel curriculum under-servers both.
  2. **Unfocuded marketing/outreach** — without segemnts, Scaler cannot target messaging (e.g. "switch to a product company", "get into a leadership track") to right audience.
  3. **No outcome benchmarking** — learners can't be told "people like you typically earns X after this course" without a notion of "people like you"
  4. **Inefficient mentor/mentee matching** — mentors are most useful when mateched to learners in a similar or one-step-ahead-sgement.
